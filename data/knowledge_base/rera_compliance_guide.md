# RERA Compliance Guide — Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016

## Overview

RERA (Real Estate Regulation and Development Act) was enacted in 2016 to protect homebuyers and promote transparency in the Indian real estate sector. Every state has its own RERA authority.

---

## Key Provisions for Buyers

### Project Registration (Section 3)
- Every real estate project with land area > 500 sqm OR > 8 apartments MUST be registered with RERA
- Developers cannot advertise, market, or sell without RERA registration
- RERA Registration Number must be displayed in all advertisements
- **Buyer Action**: Always verify RERA registration on the state RERA website

### Carpet Area Definition (Section 2)
- RERA mandates sale based on CARPET AREA (net usable area)
- Carpet area excludes: external walls, service shafts, balcony, terrace
- This prevents misleading super built-up area pricing
- **Buyer Action**: Insist on price-per-carpet-area comparison

### Advance Payment Cap (Section 13)
- Developer cannot accept more than 10% of property cost as advance/booking amount
- This is before execution of the Agreement for Sale
- **Buyer Action**: Do not pay more than 10% before agreement registration

### Defect Liability (Section 14)
- Developer is liable for structural defects for 5 YEARS after possession
- Must rectify defects within 30 days of complaint
- If not rectified, compensation is payable to the buyer
- **Buyer Action**: Document and report any defects within the 5-year window

### Completion Timeline (Section 18)
- Developer must deliver possession by the date specified in the agreement
- If delayed: buyer can either withdraw (with interest refund) or stay and receive monthly interest
- Interest rate: SBI MCLR + 2% (typically 10-12% per annum)
- **Buyer Action**: Note the RERA-registered delivery date and track progress

---

## Key Provisions for Investors

### Fund Diversion Protection (Section 4)
- 70% of collected funds must be deposited in a separate escrow account
- Can only be used for land and construction costs of THAT project
- Prevents fund diversion to other projects
- **Investor Relevance**: Reduces project completion risk

### Title & Encumbrance Verification
- Developer must declare clear title and no encumbrances
- Must provide documents: title deed, layout approval, building permit, commencement certificate
- **Investor Action**: Verify all documents independently through legal counsel

### Quarterly Project Updates
- Developers must update project status quarterly on the RERA website
- Includes: construction progress, financial statements, approvals obtained
- **Investor Action**: Monitor project progress on state RERA portal

## Penalties Under RERA

### For Developers
- Non-registration: Up to 10% of project cost
- Misleading advertisement: Up to 10% of project cost
- Non-compliance with RERA order: Imprisonment up to 3 years
- Violation of Section 4 (fund diversion): Up to 10% of project cost

### For Real Estate Agents
- Operating without registration: ₹10,000 per day of violation
- Non-compliance: Imprisonment up to 1 year

---

## RERA's Impact on Investment Decisions

1. **Reduced Risk**: RERA registration ensures basic due diligence is done
2. **Transparency**: Carpet area pricing eliminates inflated super built-up numbers
3. **Accountability**: 5-year defect liability protects asset quality
4. **Timeline Certainty**: Delivery commitments are legally binding
5. **Financial Safety**: Escrow account prevents fund diversion

**Investment Guideline**: Always invest in RERA-registered projects. Non-RERA projects carry significantly higher risk and may face legal complications during resale.
