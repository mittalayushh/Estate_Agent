# India Real Estate Market Overview — 2024-2025

## National Market Summary

The Indian real estate market has shown robust growth in 2024, with residential sales across top 7 cities reaching record highs. The sector is projected to reach $1 trillion by 2030.

### Key National Indicators
- **GDP Growth**: India's GDP growth of 6.5-7% continues to support real estate demand
- **Home Loan Interest Rates**: RBI repo rate at 6.50%, home loan rates range 8.35-9.50%
- **FDI in Real Estate**: ₹5.3 billion in FY2024, showing sustained global investor confidence
- **RERA Registrations**: Over 1,10,000 projects registered nationwide as of 2024

## City-Wise Market Analysis

### Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR)
- **Average Price**: ₹12,000-45,000 per sq ft depending on micro-market
- **YoY Appreciation**: 8-12% in premium locations (South Mumbai, BKC, Powai)
- **Demand Drivers**: Financial capital status, metro expansion (Line 3), coastal road project
- **Key Micro-Markets**: Worli (₹40,000+/sqft), Thane (₹8,000-15,000/sqft), Navi Mumbai (₹7,000-14,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~18 months of unsold inventory, down from 24 months in 2022
- **Outlook**: BULLISH — Infrastructure push and limited land driving appreciation

### Delhi NCR (Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon/Gurugram, Ghaziabad)
- **Average Price**: ₹4,000-30,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 15-25% in Noida & Gurgaon premium segments
- **Demand Drivers**: IT/ITES corridor, Jewar Airport, expressway connectivity
- **Key Micro-Markets**: Gurgaon Golf Course Road (₹20,000+/sqft), Noida Expressway (₹6,000-12,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~14 months in Gurgaon, ~20 months in Noida
- **Outlook**: STRONG GROWTH — Jewar Airport effect boosting Noida, Gurgaon continues premium appeal

### Bengaluru (Bangalore)
- **Average Price**: ₹5,000-18,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 5-10% across most micro-markets
- **Demand Drivers**: IT capital of India, startup ecosystem, metro Phase 2
- **Key Micro-Markets**: Whitefield (₹6,000-10,000/sqft), Electronic City (₹4,500-7,000/sqft), Hebbal (₹8,000-14,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~12 months, healthy absorption rate
- **Outlook**: STABLE GROWTH — Steady IT demand, new metro connectivity improving peripheral areas

### Hyderabad
- **Average Price**: ₹4,000-15,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 10-18% in western corridor
- **Demand Drivers**: IT growth (HITEC City, Gachibowli), pharma sector, relatively affordable
- **Key Micro-Markets**: Gachibowli (₹7,000-12,000/sqft), Kondapur (₹6,000-9,000/sqft), Shamshabad (₹4,000-6,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~10 months, strong absorption
- **Outlook**: HIGH GROWTH — Best value proposition among top metros

### Pune
- **Average Price**: ₹4,000-14,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 6-12%
- **Demand Drivers**: IT/auto industry hub, education center, proximity to Mumbai
- **Key Micro-Markets**: Hinjewadi (₹5,500-8,000/sqft), Kharadi (₹6,000-10,000/sqft), Wakad (₹5,000-7,500/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~14 months
- **Outlook**: POSITIVE — Affordable alternative to Mumbai, strong end-user demand

### Chennai
- **Average Price**: ₹4,000-14,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 5-8%
- **Demand Drivers**: Auto corridor, IT exports, port infrastructure
- **Key Micro-Markets**: OMR (₹5,000-9,000/sqft), Anna Nagar (₹8,000-14,000/sqft), Tambaram (₹3,500-5,500/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~16 months
- **Outlook**: MODERATE — Steady demand, limited speculative activity

### Kolkata
- **Average Price**: ₹3,000-10,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 3-6%
- **Demand Drivers**: Affordable entry point, metro expansion, IT/ITES growth
- **Key Micro-Markets**: New Town Rajarhat (₹4,000-7,000/sqft), EM Bypass (₹5,000-9,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~20 months
- **Outlook**: STABLE — Affordable market with gradual appreciation

### Ahmedabad
- **Average Price**: ₹3,000-10,000 per sq ft
- **YoY Appreciation**: 8-15% in western corridor
- **Demand Drivers**: GIFT City, industrial growth, metro connectivity
- **Key Micro-Markets**: SG Highway (₹5,000-8,000/sqft), GIFT City vicinity (₹6,000-10,000/sqft)
- **Inventory Status**: ~15 months
- **Outlook**: POSITIVE — GIFT City driving premium demand

## Property Type Benchmarks

### Apartments
- Most liquid property type in India
- Average rental yield: 2.5-3.5% in metros
- Best for: First-time buyers, investors seeking liquidity
- Risk: LOW to MODERATE

### Independent Houses / Villas
- Higher appreciation potential in suburban locations
- Lower liquidity, longer selling cycle
- Average rental yield: 1.5-2.5%
- Best for: Long-term wealth building, self-occupation
- Risk: MODERATE

### Commercial (Office/Retail)
- Higher rental yields: 6-9%
- Longer lease terms, stable income
- Best for: Portfolio diversification
- Risk: MODERATE to HIGH (market cycle sensitive)

## Investment Metrics Reference

### Stamp Duty by State (2024)
- Maharashtra: 5% (6% for Mumbai) + 1% metro cess
- Karnataka: 5% + 1% surcharge
- Telangana: 4%
- Tamil Nadu: 7%
- Delhi: 4-6% (gender-based)
- Uttar Pradesh: 5-7%

### Capital Gains Tax
- STCG (< 2 years): As per income tax slab
- LTCG (> 2 years): 20% with indexation benefit
- Section 54: Exemption on reinvestment in residential property

### ROI Benchmarks (Annual)
- Conservative (Tier 2 cities): 5-8% appreciation + 2-3% rental
- Moderate (Tier 1 suburbs): 8-12% appreciation + 2.5-3.5% rental
- Aggressive (Premium metros): 12-20% appreciation + 1.5-2.5% rental
